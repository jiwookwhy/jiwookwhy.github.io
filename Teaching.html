  • Derivatives

    Terms: Fall 2024, Fall 2023, Spring 2023, Fall 2022

    Course Description: Course covers all major aspects of the derivatives market including the pricing, hedging, and risk management use cases of options, futures, forwards, and swaps. Students engage with case studies featuring real-world applications of derivatives for speculation and portfolio hedging. Topics covered include Black-Scholes, binomial trees, put-call parity, and other foundational concepts for derivatives pricing and hedging.

    Instructors: Douglas Breeden, Paymon Khorrami

  • Fixed Income Securities

    Terms: Spring 2025, Spring 2024, Fall 2022

    Course Description: This course covers methods to valuing and modeling the risk exposures of fixed income securities and their derivatives with a view towards financial risk management setting. The course is divided into three parts, covering the basics of fixed income securities, fixed income derivatives with a focus on popular interest rate models, and credit and prepayment risk of specific fixed income securities.

    Instructors: David Hsieh, Douglas Breeden

  • Global Asset Allocation

    Terms: Spring 2023, Spring 2021

    Course Description: An investments course in applied global asset allocation with a focus on global equity and bond markets. Course engages with fundamental analysis of stock prices, earnings and dividends globally for different countries, as well as for different industry sectors in the US. Various models of equity and rates market risks are presented (e.g. CAPM, term structure models of interest rates).

    Instructors: Douglas Breeden

  • Financial Risk Management

    Terms: Spring 2025, Summer 2024, Summer 2023

    Course Description: Course covers fundamentals of risk management with particular emphasis on market risk and credit risk. Topics include theories of portfolio diversification, hedging using derivatives, and credit risk in fixed income securities. Various risk metrics such as value-at-risk (VaR) and expected shortfall (ES), exposure at default (EAD), and probability of default (PD) are explored.

    Instructors: David Hsieh

  • Financial Management

    Course Description: An introduction to the fundamental principles of asset valuation and financing in competitive financial markets. The course is aimed at an audience working in finance and adjacent industries.

    Instructors: Alon Brav

  • Finance Data Analytics

    Terms: Spring 2025, Fall 2024

    Course Description: Statistical analysis, modeling, and simulations of risk models. Evaluation of these models is conducted through the use of financial risk metrics such as value-at-risk (VaR) and expected shortfall (ES).

    Instructors: David Hsieh
